  • 2025.04 - 2026.04
    ESI-Curvature Modeling of Egg Trajectories: Morphological Effects
    Led a team to invent and design an egg rolling test rig, initially collecting 90 egg samples from various locations. Fixed a high-speed camera position, releasing each egg through a lift gate at the same initial velocity to capture video. Innovatively converted 3D motion into 2D planar motion. Utilized MATLAB, based on Kalman filtering and its extended forms, to extract dynamic behavioral features of eggs from video sequences (e.g., trajectory, instantaneous velocity, acceleration, angular velocity, wobble patterns).
    • The project has been approved at the university level and is pushing for inclusion in the National Undergraduate Innovation and Entrepreneurship Training Program.
  • 2025.05 - 2026.05
    Machine Vision for Coupled Egg Dynamics & Intelligent Sorting
    Led project members in conducting a literature review on the coupling mechanism of egg morphology, contact interface characteristics, dynamic rolling behavior, and intelligent sorting prediction. Systematically summarized 240 recent review articles in the field, and drafted an initial review manuscript.
    • Laid the groundwork for future projects such as developing egg dynamics models and establishing egg grading and recognition systems.
  • 2024.05 - 2024.08
    Research on Multi-Object Localization of Rocket Debris
    Developed a rocket debris recovery strategy using sonic booms by optimizing WGS84-based tri-sphere and quad-sphere intersection models. Employed MATLAB, including 'lsqnonlin' and a novel traversal algorithm, to implement a weighted least squares correction model. This approach, validated by Monte Carlo simulations, significantly enhanced localization accuracy and confirmed the necessity of at least four devices.
    • Outputs: Publish the paper titled “Research on Multi-Object Localization of Rocket Debris”.
